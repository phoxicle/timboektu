What's all the hubbub?

Because buying books from people near you should be easy.

Imagine: You've just registered for a new class, and now you need to get the course book. Since you're a poor student, you go to Amazon and purchase a Like New copy for three quarters the price of a new one and get it shipped to your house. Little did you know, however, that the guy you talked to at the borrel yesterday would have given you his old copy in exchange for two beers.

Now that we've got you in the mood, we introduce you to TimBoekTU. TimBoekTU is an online bulletin board for finding and selling books on your campus. There are no user accounts; just visit the website and search for the book you want. Got a old book you don't want anymore? Just click sell and fill out the form. Easy as appeltaartje.

TimBoekTU is completely non-profit and the product of volunteers. See how you can help out!

Buying books

Go to the search page and type in some keywords. The search engine will search listed book titles, authors, descriptions, courses, and ISBNs.

Once you've found a book you want, you can click on the button to contact the seller. This will simply open your default email client so that you can send them a friendly email and arrange to meet in person on campus to perform the transaction.

Buying Tips: Meet sellers in a public place, for instance in the canteen on your campus. Pay with either cash or bank transaction. If using the latter, we recommend bringing both of your laptops so you can verify the transaction went through immediately. TimBoekTU claims no responsibility for dishonest buyers or sellers.

Selling books

To sell a course book, go to the sell page and fill out the form with your book's info. The new listing will then show up in search results. After creating the new book listing, you'll receive an Edit link via email which you can use to edit the listing details should you want to do so. Interest buyers will be able to contact you via email. And don't worry about receiving spam from bots on the web -- if you look at the HTML source of listing detail, you'll notice that your email address is in fact encrypted ;)

Old listings are periodically deleted from the server. When that time is nearing for your listing, you'll receive an email asking if you want to renew your listing.

Selling Tips: The more info your provide in your listing the more likely someone will buy your book. Including an exact price will also help your chances, because buyers will generally avoid bargaining if they can.
